📱 Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

The Do's (Best Practices)

Do audit your privacy settings: Regularly check who can see your posts, photos, and personal information across all platforms.

Do think before you post: Assume everything you post is permanent and could be seen by future employers, internship coordinators, or professors.

Do use platforms for networking: Follow industry leaders, join professional groups on LinkedIn, and connect with college alumni.

Do verify information before sharing: Fact-check news and announcements before retweeting or sharing to avoid spreading misinformation.

Do engage respectfully: Treat online interactions just like face-to-face conversations; be polite and constructive in comments and debates.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Don'ts (Risks to Avoid)

Don't overshare sensitive information: Never post your exact real-time location, college ID card, phone number, or travel itineraries.

Don't vent about your college or professors publicly: Complaining about courses, faculty, or peers online leaves a highly unprofessional digital footprint.

Don't engage in cyberbullying or trolling: Never participate in online harassment, hate speech, or sharing malicious rumors.

Don't post inappropriate content: Avoid posting photos or videos involving illegal activities, underage drinking, or anything that violates your university's code of conduct.

Don't plagiarize content: Do not pass off other people's artwork, writing, or coding snippets as your own without proper credit and permission.
